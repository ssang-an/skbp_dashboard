## 2. Criterion Details

### Target Relevance

- Score: 3/3
- Evidence Type: E4_asset_specific_clinical_evidence
- Reason: KCNT1 gain-of-function epilepsy is a direct SKBP E/I Balance / Ion Channel fit.
- Evidence type reason: Asset-specific clinical trial and official pipeline sources directly link ABS-1230 to KCNT1-related epilepsy; disease biology sources support KCNT1 gain-of-function ion-channelopathy as causal epilepsy biology.
- Why not higher: Already maximum score.
- Uncertain points:
- Clinical relevance beyond KCNT1-positive epilepsy into broader genetic epilepsies remains unproven.

### Competitive Landscape

- Score: 2/3
- Evidence Type: E4_asset_specific_clinical_evidence
- Reason: No approved KCNT1-targeted therapy exists, but direct same-target programs are active.
- Evidence type reason: Same-target clinical competitor S230815 and broader DEE clinical competitors are publicly visible; Atalanta ATL-201 is preclinical but asset-specific.
- Why not higher: At least one direct same-target Phase 1b/2 program and one preclinical RNAi competitor are visible.
- Uncertain points:
- S230815 modality is described as ASO by KCNT1 Foundation; official Servier page emphasizes intrathecal delivery and KCNT1 activity blocking.
- No head-to-head clinical efficacy or safety data exist.

### MoA Validity

- Score: 3/3
- Evidence Type: E3_asset_specific_preclinical_or_technical_evidence
- Reason: MoA is strongly validated preclinically and mechanistically coherent with KCNT1 gain-of-function disease.
- Evidence type reason: Asset-specific poster provides quantitative in vitro potency, mutant coverage, selectivity profiling, ADME/CNS penetration, and in vivo seizure reduction.
- Why not higher: Already maximum score; however, clinical pharmacodynamic seizure reduction in patients is not yet public.
- Uncertain points:
- Data are from company/AES poster rather than peer-reviewed full manuscript.
- Long-term pediatric KCNT1 inhibition safety remains uncertain.

### Platform Attractiveness

- Score: 2/3
- Evidence Type: E3_asset_specific_preclinical_or_technical_evidence
- Reason: Oral small molecule CNS ion-channel pharmacology is attractive, but broader platform validation remains limited.
- Evidence type reason: Asset-specific evidence supports oral CNS exposure and selectivity; broader platform reproducibility is supported mainly by company pipeline and approach pages.
- Why not higher: No broad independent validation of the platform engine or multiple successful clinical translations was found.
- Uncertain points:
- No head-to-head comparison versus intrathecal ASO/siRNA delivery approaches.
- The extent of proprietary computational/data-science differentiation is not deeply disclosed.

### Expansion Potential

- Score: 2/3
- Evidence Type: E1_company_claim_or_scientific_rationale_only
- Reason: Expansion beyond KCNT1-related epilepsy is plausible but not yet clinically validated.
- Evidence type reason: Expansion into other genetic epilepsies is stated on company pages and financing release, but public asset-specific expansion data are limited.
- Why not higher: Expansion evidence is currently company-claim/scientific rationale rather than asset-specific proof in non-KCNT1 epilepsy.
- Uncertain points:
- Whether KCNT1 inhibition benefits broader genetic epilepsies is unproven.
- Expansion label, patient selection, and regulatory path are unclear.

### Data Maturity

- Score: 2/3
- Evidence Type: E4_asset_specific_clinical_evidence
- Reason: Clinical-stage with strong preclinical package, but no public patient efficacy data yet.
- Evidence type reason: ABS-1230 has IND clearance, Phase 1a human safety/tolerability disclosure, and Phase 1b/2 patient study initiation, but no patient efficacy readout.
- Why not higher: No public Phase 1b/2 efficacy readout, long-term pediatric safety, or patient PK/PD exposure-response results.
- Uncertain points:
- ClinicalTrials.gov page rendering was limited in search output, but NCT ID and trial details were cross-checked against Actio and registry snippets.
- Detailed Phase 1a data package is not fully public.

### Marketability

- Score: 1/3
- Evidence Type: E2_indirect_or_class_level_evidence
- Reason: A. TAP ~800 U.S. targetable patients; B. Unrisked Peak Sales ~USD 107.5M; C. Obtainable Peak Sales ~USD 100.0M.
- Evidence type reason: Market model uses public prevalence, rare epilepsy analog pricing, ICER launch-price context, and competitor assumptions; no asset-specific pricing or sales data exist.
- Why not higher: Even with annual net price of USD 300k, 45% peak penetration, and expansion adjustment, obtainable peak sales remain around USD 100M.
- Uncertain points:
- True diagnosed prevalence and global commercially accessible prevalence are uncertain.
- Actual ABS-1230 net price is unknown.
- Broader genetic epilepsy expansion could materially change the market model if clinically validated.
- U.S.-anchored model may understate global upside but avoids unsupported global extrapolation.
