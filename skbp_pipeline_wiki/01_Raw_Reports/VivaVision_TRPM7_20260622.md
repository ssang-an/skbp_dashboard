## 4) Criterion Detail Pages

### 4.1 Target Relevance
Score: **2 / 3**

Main line:  
TRPM7은 ion channel + kinase라는 독특한 bifunctional target으로 CNS 질환과 연결되는 근거가 있으나, 질환별로 “억제해야 하는가 / 활성화해야 하는가”가 달라질 수 있어 target hypothesis 정리가 먼저 필요합니다.

What was checked:
- Target identity
- Disease/biology relevance
- SKBP Theme / Cluster fit
- General neurodegeneration / neuroinflammation / epilepsy relevance

Evidence trail:
- NCBI Gene는 TRPM7을 “ion channel and serine/threonine protein kinase”로 설명하며, intracellular calcium 증가와 magnesium ion homeostasis 조절에 관여한다고 설명합니다. [10]
- Nature Communications 2023은 TRPM7이 divalent cation balance, immune responses, cell mobility/proliferation/differentiation에 관여하고 neuronal/cardiovascular disorders, tumor progression과 관련되어 drug target으로 부상했다고 설명합니다. [11]
- Journal of Neuroinflammation 2024 status epilepticus 모델에서 TRPM7 발현 증가, TRPM7 knockdown 및 NS8593 inhibition이 neuronal damage, pyroptosis, hyperexcitability를 완화했다는 결과가 제시되었습니다. [12]
- Science Signaling 2023 AD mouse model에서는 TRPM7 kinase activity가 amyloid-β degradation을 유도해 synaptic/cognitive deficits를 회복한다는 방향의 결과가 제시되어, 단순 antagonist 전략은 AD에서는 오히려 역방향일 수 있습니다. [15]

Investigation note:
- 3점이 아닌 이유: TRPM7이 CNS와 연결되지만, target directionality가 질환별로 다릅니다. Stroke/status epilepticus에서는 inhibition 논리가 가능하지만, AD에서는 kinase activity enhancement가 보호적일 수 있습니다.
- 1점이 아닌 이유: ion channel target으로서 구조생물학과 disease model evidence가 모두 존재합니다.

Uncertain points:
- VivaVision의 TRPM7 asset이 실제 CNS indication을 목표로 하는지 확인 필요.
- TRPM7 channel inhibition과 kinase inhibition 중 어느 domain을 조절하는지 확인 필요.
- BBB penetration이 필요한 CNS systemic drug인지, topical ophthalmology drug인지 확인 필요.

### 4.3 MoA Validity
Score: **2 / 3**

Main line:  
TRPM7 inhibition has credible target-level structural and in vivo disease-model support, but company asset-level proof is missing and disease-directionality risk is material.

What was checked:
- Journal publication / PMID / DOI
- Mechanistic consistency
- Functional readout
- Disease linkage
- Safety-relevant signal

Evidence trail:
- Nature Communications 2023 used cryo-EM, functional analysis and MD simulations to identify mechanisms of TRPM7 activation/inhibition and reported inhibitor binding that stabilizes TRPM7 closed state. [11]
- Cell Reports 2024 reported selective TRPM7 inhibition by CCT128930 and described a vanilloid-like site / closed-state stabilization mechanism. [13]
- Journal of Neuroinflammation 2024 showed p-STAT3/TRPM7/Zn2+/p-STAT3 feedback in status epilepticus and mitigation by TRPM7 inhibition or knockdown. [12]
- JGP 2024 identified VPC01091.4 as a potent TRPM7 channel activity inhibitor with anti-inflammatory effects in mice, but noted anti-inflammatory effects are not solely dependent on TRPM7. [14]
- Science Signaling 2023 AD paper suggests TRPM7 kinase activity can be protective via Aβ degradation, creating context-specific risk for blanket antagonism. [15]

Investigation note:
- 2점 이상 근거: publication-level structural and in vivo evidence is visible.
- 3점이 아닌 이유: no asset-specific potency/selectivity/PK/PD/human biomarker data for VivaVision TRPM7 program; target modulation direction may vary by disease.

Uncertain points:
- Channel-only inhibitor인지, kinase inhibitor인지, bifunctional modulator인지 확인 필요.
- TRPM6 selectivity and divalent cation homeostasis safety margin 확인 필요.
- CNS indication일 경우 BBB penetration and brain exposure 필요.

### 4.5 Expansion Potential
Score: **2 / 3**

Main line:  
TRPM7은 CNS, ophthalmology, oncology, cardiovascular, immune/inflammation으로 확장 가능성이 있으나, disease별 biology direction이 달라 indiscriminate expansion은 위험합니다.

What was checked:
- Expansion beyond main indication
- Same biology/platform reuse
- Adjacent indications
- Multiple assets from same platform

Evidence trail:
- Market-source profile explicitly maps TRPM7 antagonism to oncology, ophthalmology, cardiovascular and CNS disorders. [8], [9]
- TRPM7 structural biology supports druggability and inhibitor design. [11], [13]
- Status epilepticus data support epilepsy/neuroinflammation hypothesis. [12]
- AD data suggest kinase activity enhancement, not inhibition, may be therapeutic, so neurodegeneration expansion must be mechanism-direction specific. [15]
- Oncology literature and natural product data suggest TRPM7 inhibition may affect proliferation/migration/invasion in certain cancer contexts. [16], [17]

Investigation note:
- 3점이 아닌 이유: expansion breadth is high, but therapeutic direction is not uniform.
- 1점이 아닌 이유: multiple disease areas and mechanistic publications exist.

Uncertain points:
- Which indication VivaVision selected first.
- Whether TRPM7 antagonist can be repositioned into CNS with adequate brain exposure.
- Whether safety liability from systemic divalent-cation homeostasis limits chronic use.

### 4.7 Marketability
Score: **1 / 3**

Main line:  
Conservative CNS acute ischemic stroke scenario gives **A. TAP ≈ 131k US patients/year**, **B. Unrisked Peak Sales ≈ USD 261M**, **C. Obtainable Peak Sales ≈ USD 125M**. This is below USD 1B and highly dependent on indication selection.

What was checked:
- Targetable addressable patient
- Unrisked peak sales
- Competition haircut
- Pricing power adjustment
- Expansion capacity adjustment

Worksheet:

| Step | What to fill | Evidence / assumption |
|---|---|---|
| A. TAP | US annual stroke pool >795,000 x ischemic stroke 87% x diagnosis/ER pathway 90% x acute treatment eligibility 30% x treatable subgroup 70% = 약 130,722 patients/year | CDC reports >795,000 US strokes/year and 87% ischemic; ASA describes acute ischemic treatment time-window constraints. Diagnosis/eligibility/subgroup rates are analyst assumptions requiring claims/registry validation. [18], [19] |
| B. Unrisked Peak Sales | TAP 130,722 x Annual Net Price USD 10,000 x Peak Penetration 20% x Treatment Duration Factor 1.0 = USD 261M | Acute one-time/short-course neuroprotectant adjunct price assumption. Alteplase CMS payment around USD 6,400 in 2014 was used as analog, with novel adjunct premium; actual price highly uncertain. [20] |
| Entry-order matrix | 3-player example: 1st ~50%, 2nd ~30%, 3rd ~20%. TRPM7 would likely be adjunct/late entrant in AIS workflow | Current AIS treatment includes alteplase/tenecteplase and thrombectomy; neuroprotective drugs have historically high failure risk. [19] |
| C. Obtainable Peak Sales | USD 261M x Competition Haircut 0.50 x Pricing Power Adjustment 0.80 x Expansion Capacity Adjustment 1.20 = USD 125M | Competition haircut reflects reperfusion SOC and crowded acute workflow; pricing adjustment reflects acute hospital cost constraints; expansion adjustment gives modest upside for SE/ophthalmology/oncology but not enough without active asset data. |
| Final score basis | 0 < weak market, 1 < USD 1,000M obtainable, 2 >= USD 1,000M, 3 >= USD 2,000M + high expansion | Obtainable peak sales is far below USD 1B in conservative US AIS scenario; score 1. |

Investigation note:
- Marketability is based on obtainable peak sales, not rNPV.
- Because the actual indication is not confirmed, this market model is a **screening scenario**, not final forecast.
- If the actual program is ophthalmology topical use, a separate glaucoma/ocular hypertension model should be run. VVN539 is the known VivaVision glaucoma clinical asset, but its disclosed MoA is ROCK inhibitor + NO donor, not TRPM7. [3], [6], [7]

Uncertain points:
- Actual lead indication.
- Route of administration and treatment duration.
- Real-world eligible population in the chosen indication.
- Pricing and reimbursement benchmark.
- Whether TRPM7 antagonist will be mono-therapy, adjunct to reperfusion, or topical ophthalmology agent.
